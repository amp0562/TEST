Writing Process

The writing process for library entries is intended to be clear and methodical. Writers begin with a broad idea, then link it to related topics like Chapter Collections and the structure of a typical encyclopedia article.

Once the content is drafted, editors verify it against the Reference Guides and make sure the article is easy to read. That often includes adding inline links to other entries such as the Catalog Entry that describes how to store the work.

See also: Chapter Collections, Reference Guides, Catalog Entry.
